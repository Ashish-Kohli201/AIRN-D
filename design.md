# AIRN'D — Design System & Brand Language

## Brand Premise

AIRN'D feels like a forgotten 1970s Italian menswear house rediscovered in a Delhi market and reinterpreted into a modern grooming brand.

It is masculine without performance.
Refined without trying to appear luxury.
Quiet without becoming minimal-for-the-sake-of-minimal.

The brand should never feel polished in a tech-startup sense.
It should feel lived in.
Handled.
Inherited.

---

# Core Emotional Tone

## What AIRN'D Should Feel Like

* Cigarette smoke caught in warm light
* A whiskey glass left half-finished
* Hotel bathrooms in old European cities
* Leather that has aged properly
* Men who look like they have made difficult decisions
* Grooming as ritual, not vanity
* Taste without explanation

## What AIRN'D Should Never Feel Like

* Clinical
* Youthful
* Hyper-clean
* Trendy
* Silicon Valley minimalism
* Wellness startup aesthetics
* Glossy influencer grooming
* Scandinavian purity
* Loud masculinity
* Gym culture

---

# Brand Voice

## Tone

AIRN'D speaks quietly.
Never over-explains.
Never sells aggressively.
Never sounds excited.

Every line should feel considered.
Like it was written by someone who removed more words than they added.

## Writing Characteristics

* Lowercase preferred in conversational copy
* Sparse punctuation
* Short sentences
* Intentional pauses
* No emojis
* No exclamation marks
* No marketing buzzwords
* No forced masculinity language

## Example Copy Tone

### Good

"made slowly. used daily."

"good taste isn't quiet. it just doesn't shout."

"for men who don't explain themselves."

### Bad

"premium grooming essentials for the modern man"

"elevate your routine"

"crafted for confidence"

# Colour System

## Primary Colour — Near Black

Used for:

* Headlines
* Navigation
* Product bottles
* Key typography
* Background blocks

### HEX

`#1A1814`

### Characteristics

* Warm undertone
* Never true black
* Feels like charcoal and leather combined
* Must absorb light rather than reflect it

---

## Secondary Colour — Raw Silver

Used for:

* Thin rules
* Secondary text
* Embossing
* Metallic detailing
* Packaging accents

### HEX

`#A8A39D`

### Characteristics

* Muted sterling silver
* Never chrome
* Never glossy
* Slightly oxidised feel

---

## Accent Colour — Bruised Amber

Used extremely sparingly.

### HEX

`#C4862B`

### Usage Rules

Allowed:

* Hover states
* Product liquid tones
* Small highlights
* Rare CTA emphasis
* Reflection lighting

Not allowed:

* Large backgrounds
* Paragraph text
* Buttons everywhere
* Gradients
* Decorative fills

Accent colour should feel earned.
Not decorative.

---

## Background Colour — Cartridge Paper

### HEX

`#F2EDE6`

### Characteristics

* Warm off-white
* Textured
* Organic
* Slightly aged
* Never pure white

Backgrounds should always include subtle grain.

# Typography System

## Headline Typeface

### Style Direction

High-contrast serif inspired by:

* Italian editorial typography
* Old fashion publishing
* Luxury print catalogues

### Recommended References

* Cormorant Garamond
* Freight Display
* Canela
* Custom Didone-inspired serif

### Typography Behaviour

* Mix heavy and hairline weights intentionally
* Use oversized scaling
* Tight kerning in large titles
* Allow negative space around headlines

### Headline Examples

AIRN'D

"good taste isn't quiet."

"made in small numbers."

---

## Body Typeface

### Style Direction

Grotesque sans serif.
Functional but elegant.

### Recommended References

* Neue Haas Grotesk
* Suisse Int'l
* Akzidenz-Grotesk
* Graphik

### Rules

* Wide tracking for labels
* Lowercase for conversational copy
* ALL CAPS only for metadata and navigation
* Never overuse bold

---

# Layout System

## Structure

AIRN'D layouts are asymmetric.
Nothing should feel templated.

Content should feel placed, not aligned.

## Principles

* Large negative space
* Offset grids
* Uneven composition
* Editorial spacing
* Thin dividing rules
* No cards
* No boxed UI
* No rounded corners

## Grid Behaviour

* Use broken-column structures
* Allow intentional imbalance
* Headlines can overflow grid boundaries
* Imagery should sometimes crop awkwardly

The page should feel curated.
Not designed by software.

---

# Texture & Surface Language

## Grain

Every surface contains subtle grain.
Nothing flat.
Nothing digitally sterile.

### Grain Sources

* Paper texture overlays
* Film noise
* Dust artifacts
* Slight underexposure
* Matte finishes

## Surface Finish Rules

### Allowed

* Matte
* Soft reflections
* Worn textures
* Oxidised materials
* Leather grain
* Stone textures
* Smoked glass

### Not Allowed

* Glossy plastics
* Neon effects
* Perfect reflections
* Glassmorphism
* Flat vector aesthetics
* Over-clean renders

---

# Photography Direction

## Core Philosophy

Photography should feel documentary.
Not campaign-driven.

The camera observes.
It does not perform.

## Subjects

* Men aged 30+
* Salt-and-pepper hair
* Weathered skin
* Strong hands
* Natural posture
* Movement over posing

## Composition Rules

* Faces partially hidden
* Side profiles preferred
* Rear angles preferred
* Available light only
* Underexposed by half a stop
* Filmic warmth

## Environments

* Bathrooms with stone and steel
* Old apartments
* Rain-darkened streets
* Tailor shops
* Hotel mirrors
* Dim interiors
* Lajpat Nagar textures mixed with Italian restraint

## Never Use

* White seamless backdrops
* Bright smiles
* Hyper-retouched skin
* Gym physiques
* Influencer-style poses
* Clinical skincare imagery

---

# Product Design Language

## Product Philosophy

AIRN'D products should look permanent.
Almost archival.

Like something discovered rather than launched.

---

## Bottle Direction

### Core Bottle

* Dark amber glass
* Thick walls
* Matte black cap
* No printed labels
* Brand mark embossed directly into glass
* Low reflection finish

### Bottle Feel

Should resemble:

* Vintage apothecary containers
* Japanese whiskey bottles
* European grooming objects from the 70s

### What It Should Never Resemble

* Supplement containers
* Tech products
* Clean skincare startups
* Bright cosmetic packaging

---

## Packaging

### Outer Packaging

* Rigid matte board
* Charcoal-black paper
* Debossed typography
* Minimal information hierarchy

### Inner Packaging

* Crumpled textured wrap paper
* Soft shadows
* Hidden reveal experience

### Finishing

Allowed:

* Embossing
* Debossing
* Foil used sparingly
* Raw paper texture

Not allowed:

* Spot UV
* High gloss coating
* Plastic shine
* Bright colour printing

---

# Motion Design System

## Motion Philosophy

AIRN'D moves slowly.
Nothing should bounce.
Nothing should feel playful.

Motion should feel like smoke.
Not software.

---

## Animation Rules

### Allowed

* Slow fades
* Gentle vertical slides
* Word-by-word text reveals
* Low-speed parallax
* Soft opacity transitions

### Not Allowed

* Bounce easing
* Elastic motion
* Fast transitions
* Over-animated interfaces
* Microinteraction overload

---

## Scroll Behaviour

* Text appears in phrases
* Images reveal gradually
* Sections breathe before transition
* Hero parallax used once only

The site should feel cinematic.
Not interactive for the sake of interaction.

---

# UI System

## Buttons

### Style

* Sharp corners
* Thin borders
* Generous spacing
* Quiet hover states

### Hover Behaviour

* Slight opacity shift
* Subtle amber tone
* No glow
* No scale bounce

---

## Navigation

* Minimal
* Thin typography
* Wide spacing
* Low visual noise
* Should disappear into the experience

---

## Dividers

Use only:

* Thin rules
* Hairline separators

Never use:

* Cards
* Containers
* Heavy section borders

---

# Iconography

## Rules

AIRN'D does not use conventional icon systems.

No filled icons.
No modern rounded UI symbols.

### Allowed

* Hand-drawn arrows
* Thin directional marks
* Minimal line indicators

### Not Allowed

* Emoji-like icons
* Material icons
* Flat illustration systems
* Rounded pictograms

---

# Digital Experience

## Homepage Structure

### Hero

* Massive typography
* One strong image
* Slow parallax
* Sparse copy

### Product Sections

* Editorial layouts
* Minimal explanation
* Heavy imagery
* Breathing room

### Storytelling

The brand should reveal itself slowly.
Never dump information.

Users should feel like they discovered AIRN'D.
Not like AIRN'D pitched itself.
