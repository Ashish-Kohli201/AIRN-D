BET 01

The Product

₹30L

Four SKUs. The Silver. The Grain. The Weight. The Salt. Not eight. Not twelve. Four. Because a brand that launches with eight products does not believe in any of them. 500 units per SKU. 2000 units total. Matte black packaging. AIRN'D embossed. No label. No ingredient list on the front. The brand is the label. Lean enough to move fast. Enough to learn what the market reaches for first.

WHY THIS NUMBER

Manufacturing and packaging at this volume runs ₹22L. The remaining ₹8L is buffer for the reorder that happens when one SKU moves faster than the others. And one always does.

BET 02

The Proof

₹35L

No advertising yet. Not one rupee. Instead, 40 men. Carefully chosen. Not beauty influencers. Not grooming pages. Men who happen to have an audience. A fitness coach in Bandra. A cinematographer in Delhi. A chef in Bangalore. An architect in Bombay. Men who look like AIRN'D customers because they are AIRN'D customers. Ship them the product. No brief. No script. No hashtag. No fee. If they use it and talk about it, that is proof the product works. If they do not, that is information we needed before spending ₹25L on ads. Seeding runs for six weeks before a single performance rupee is spent.

WHY THIS NUMBER

40 creators at an average product value of ₹3,500 per kit plus courier is ₹6L. The remaining ₹29L covers a dedicated seeding manager for six months whose only job is to find the right 40 men and build a real relationship with each one.

BET 03

The Pull

₹25L

Performance marketing starts only after seeding has run for six weeks. Instagram Reels and YouTube pre-roll only. One creative per platform. Not ten variations. One. Because if the one does not work, ten will not either. That is a positioning problem, not a creative problem. Target: men 26 to 38. Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore first. Interest signals around fitness, style, travel, cinema. Not grooming. Men who care about how they look but do not think of themselves as grooming people.

WHY THIS NUMBER

Six weeks of paid at this budget gives us enough data to know if the creative converts. If cost per acquisition is above ₹800 after six weeks we stop and rebuild the creative. We do not spend the second half of this budget on a broken funnel.

BET 04

The Voice

₹10L

One hire. Not an agency. A person. A man in his late 20s or early 30s who is the AIRN'D customer and can write. He runs the Instagram. He writes the emails. He responds to every DM personally for the first six months. The brand voice is one human voice or it is nothing.

WHY THIS NUMBER

Six month contract at ₹1.5L per month plus content production budget. This person is not a social media manager. He is the brand's first ambassador and the customer's first point of contact. Those are not agency functions. They are founder functions until the founder can do them himself.

WHY THIS ORDER

Product before proof. Proof before pull. Pull before voice.

This sequence is not accidental. Most D2C brands reverse it. They spend on ads before they know if the product converts. They build an audience before they have something worth building an audience around. We do not spend on pull until creators have told us the product earns it. We do not hire a voice until we know what the brand sounds like in the wild. Every rupee in this plan unlocks the next one. Remove any step and the whole sequence breaks.

The biggest risk in year one is not running out of money.

It is spending it before you know what works.

THAT IS HOW WE SPEND YEAR ONE.
